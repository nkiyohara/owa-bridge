# Code of conduct

## Our standard

Project spaces should be safe, respectful, and technically rigorous. Be
considerate of different backgrounds and experience levels, critique ideas and
code rather than people, and make room for questions and correction.

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, deliberate
intimidation, doxxing, and publication of another person's mailbox, identity,
tenant, credential, or session data are unacceptable.

## Scope

This standard applies in repository discussions, reviews, issue trackers,
release channels, and other spaces where someone represents the project.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner. Do not put personal
or security-sensitive evidence in a public issue. The owner may edit or remove
content, close discussions, restrict participation, or ban contributors when
needed to protect people or the project.

Reports will be reviewed in context and handled as confidentially as practical.
Good-faith security reports and respectful technical disagreement are not
conduct violations.
